02.5 — Risk Profiles

Not every at-risk student needs the same email.

At-risk students are clustered by behavioral and demographic similarity into three profiles. Each one tells a different story about why a student is disengaging — and points to a different retention strategy.

Time-Constrained

Availability-driven drop-off. Often full-time workers or parents.

Disengaged Learner

Passive viewing without active participation. Often younger, mobile-primary.

Quiet Decliner

Was engaged, recently dropped off. High investment, highly recoverable.

03 — Student Roster

The students who need intervention.

Every at-risk student is matched to one of three behavioral profiles — each with a distinct, tailored retention strategy. Not every dropout risk looks the same, and the right outreach depends on knowing which kind of risk you're looking at.

04 — System Design

Three layers. One contract.

1

Data Layer

Synthetic telemetry generated with NumPy — mimicking real EdTech signals including seasonal churn and ghost-student edge cases.

generator.py → students.csv
2

Intelligence Layer

Random Forest classifier trained on engineered features. Recall ≥ 0.80 as the primary metric — catching dropouts matters more than eliminating false alarms.

model.py → model.joblib
3

Visualization Layer

GSAP scroll narrative reads a single JSON contract. The frontend knows nothing about Python — it only reads predictions.json.

notifier.py → predictions.json
